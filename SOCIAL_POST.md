## LINKEDIN POST

I'm the IT advisor for the SS Jeremiah O'Brien — a 1943 Liberty Ship operating as a seagoing museum in San Francisco under USCG Subchapter H.

I've watched the crew track drills, inspections, and certificates with spreadsheets and paper logbooks. A missed fire drill means a $25,000 fine or Port State Control detention. When the chief engineer retires, the institutional knowledge goes with him.

There's no software built for this. So I built it.

**Barbary Coast Marine** is an AI compliance officer for commercial vessels. You tell it what happened in plain English — "we ran the fire drill this morning, 12 crew participated" — and it:

→ Writes a structured USCG logbook entry with the 46 CFR citation
→ Clears the compliance flag on the dashboard
→ Monitors USCG safety bulletins and Port State Control focus areas in real time
→ Sends email alerts before items go critical

106 hand-written USCG rules across six subchapters. Offline-first architecture — designed to run on a vessel's own hardware without satellite internet.

First pilot: the O'Brien herself, Memorial Day cruise, May 30th. It ran live at sea.

Built for the BuilderShip Hackathon using Nebius AI, Tavily, Composio, and OpenClaw.

Try the live demo: demo.barbarycoastmarine.com (login: captain / obrien2026)
